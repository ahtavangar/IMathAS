Macro Library complex

Library name: complex

Functions to handle operations for complex numbers.  Version 1.  October 2020

Author:  Amir Tavangar



To import the library, include loadlibrary("complex") in the question's common control.

The functions in this library can be used to handle operations for complex numbers such as

  • Converting from standard to polar form and vice versa
  • Finding modulus, argument, and conjugate
  • Multiplication, division, raising to a power, and taking the nth root
  • Finding real or complex roots of quadratic equations

List of functions:

  • cx_modul: Modulus
  • cx_argRad: Argument in radian
  • cx_prettyargRad: Argument as a multiple of pi
  • cx_argDeg: Argument in degree
  • cx_conj: Conjugate
  • cx_polDeg: Polar form (degree)
  • cx_prettypolDeg: Polar form with trigs (Deg)
  • cx_polRad: Polar form (radian)
  • cx_prettypolRad: Polar form with trigs (Rad)
  • cx_polEu: Polar-Euler notation
  • cx_real: Real part
  • cx_img: Imaginary part
  • cx_std: Standard form
  • cx_div: Division
  • cx_mul: Multiplication
  • cx_pow: Power
  • cx_root: Root
  • cx_quadRoot: Quadratic roots

    cx_modul(Re, Im, [roundto=3])

    Function cx_modul returns the modulus (absolute value) of a complex number, which is sqrt(Re^2 + Im^2).

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.
    • roundto: Optional - number of decimal places to which modulus should be rounded off; the default is 3 decimal places.

    Returns:

    The modulus of a complex number.

     

    cx_argRad(Re, Im)

     Function: cx_argRad returns the argument (angle theta) of a complex number in radian.

     Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    The argument of a complex number in radian.

     

    cx_prettyargRad(Re, Im)

     Function cx_prettyargRad returns the argument (angle theta) of a complex number in radian as a multiple of pi in a string format.

     Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    The argument of a complex number in radian as a multiple of pi.

     

    cx_argDeg(Re, Im)

     Function cx_argDeg returns the argument (angle theta) of a complex number in degree.

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    The argument of a complex number in degree.

     

    cx_conj(Re, Im)

     Function cx_conj returns the conjugate of a complex number; the conjugate of (a+bi) is (a-bi).

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

     The conjugate of a complex number.

     

    cx_polDeg(Re, Im)

    Function cx_polDeg converts the standard form to polar form and returns the modulus and the argument (degree) as a paired value.

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    The modulus and the argument, degree, of a complex number as a paired value.

     

    cx_prettypolDeg(Re, Im)

    Function cx_prettypolDeg returns polar form of a complex number in the form of z = r(cos(t) + i sin(t)) as a string. It can be used as an answer in Calculated-Complex question type with $answerformat = "sloppycomplex".

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    Polar form of a complex number with the angle in degree as a string.

     

    cx_polRad(Re, Im)

    Function cx_polRad converts the standard form to polar form and returns the modulus and the argument (radian) as a paired values.

     Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    The modulus and the argument, in radian, of a complex number as a paired values.

     

    cx_prettypolRad(Re, Im)

    Function cx_prettypolRad returns polar form of a complex number in the form of z = r(cos(t) + i sin(t)) as a string. It can be used as an answer in Calculated-Complex question type with $answerformat = "sloppycomplex".

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    Polar form of a complex number with the angle in radian as a string.

     

    cx_polEu(Re, Im)

    Function cx_polEu returns the polar form of a complex number with the Euler's formula notation: z = r e^i*theta as a string.

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.

     Returns:

    The polar form with the Euler's formula notation with angle in radian.

     

    cx_real(mod, arg)

     Function cx_real converts the polar form to standard form and returns the real part of the complex number.

    Parameters:

    • mod: The modulus of a complex number.
    • arg: The argument (angle theta) of a complex number in degree.

     Returns:

    The real part of the complex number.

     

    cx_img(mod, arg)

    Function cx_img converts the polar form to standard form and returns the imaginary part of the complex number.

    Parameters:

    • mod: The modulus of a complex number.
    • arg: The argument (angle theta) of a complex number in degree.

     Returns:

    The imaginary part of the complex number.

     

    cx_std(mod, arg)

     Function cx_std converts the polar form and returns the complex number in standard form z = a + bi as a string.

     Parameters:

    • mod: The modulus of a complex number.
    • arg: The argument (angle theta) of a complex number in degree.

     Returns:

    The complex number in standard form as a string.

     

    cx_div(nums)

     Function cx_div returns the quotient of two complex numbers in standard form z = a + bi as a string.

     Parameters:

    • nums: An array of real and imaginary parts of two complex numbers given in lists. For example, to divide -2-3i by sqrt(3)-i, nums = array("-2,-3", "sqrt(3),-1").

     Returns:

    The quotient of two complex numbers in standard form.

     

    cx_mul(nums)

     Function cx_mul returns the product of input complex numbers in standard form z = a + bi as a string.

     Parameters:

    • nums: An array of real and imaginary parts of complex numbers given in lists. For example, to multiply 2-3i, -5i, and sqrt(2)-i, nums = array("2,-3", "0,-5", "sqrt(2),-1").

     Returns:

    The product of input complex numbers in standard form.

     

    cx_pow(Re, Im, exp)

     Function cx_pow returns the power of a complex number in standard form z = a + bi as a string.

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.
    • exp: exponent to which the complex number is raised.

     Returns:

     The power of a complex number in the standard form.

     

    cx_root(Re, Im, n)

     Function cx_root returns an array of the nth roots of a complex number in standard form z = a + bi. Use question type Complex where $answer = ”A[0] , ..., A[n-1]” and $answerformat = "list".

    Parameters:

    • Re: Real part of a complex number.
    • Im: Imaginary part of a complex number.
    • n: The nth roots. For example, for the cubic roots n=3.

     Returns:

    An array of the nth roots of a complex number in the standard form.

     

    cx_quadRoot(a, b, c)

    Function: cx_quadRoot returns an array of roots of the quadratic equation f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c. Real roots are returned as an A = array(root1,root2) ordered from the smallest to largest, and the complex roots are returned as an A = array(a+bi, a-bi). Question type Complex handles both types of solutions where $answer = ”A[0] , A[1]” and $answerformat = "list".

     Parameters:

    • a: The numerical coefficient of x^2
    • b: The numerical coefficient of x
    • c: The constant

     Returns:

    An array of the two roots (either real or complex) of a quadratic equation.
